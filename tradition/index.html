Egypten traditions


Traditions in Egypt are a little different and interesting. If you are invited to the house of a person in this country and you are offered a glass of juice, remember to drink all of the juice to the end. It is customary that if a guest doesn’t finish their glass of juice that they offered by a host family with daughters, then these girls won’t get married.

One of the other interesting traditions in Egypt is Marakbiya. It means that they should not be taken seriously when they invite you to eat, drink or something else when he really doesn't mean it.

In Egypt, people use el hons to mince garlic. It is customary to celebrate the baby’s seven days old. In this tradition, the guest sings loud songs, and the baby’s parents distributings sweets to the guests. The singer asks the baby to listen to his/her parents and always be a calm and worthy child. The Egyptians believe that the baby follows the statements they say in its ears, and applies them in the future life.

You must be careful when you go into a new place, like a new house, step in with the right foot. This current is a sign of respect and brings in goods, happiness and prosperity.

There are many festivals and events that Egyptians celebrate with muslims and christians living in harmony. One of the events is Coptic Christmas on the 7th of january. Because of secularism and its equality, Easter and Christmas are both national holidays in Egypt. These festivities are celebrated especially in Cairo and the Copticregions of Egypt.

All across the world the most important month in the year for muslims is Ramadan which muslims avoid eating food, drinks and cigarettes and fasting for a whole month. Egypt is also celebrating Ramadan which is really beautiful. They celebrate their culture with their family and friends.

According to ancient Egyptian mythology, the sun god was the most important deity for the ancient egyptians. The festival is celebrated in February and October at the largest temple in the ancient world at Abu Simbel when the sun rays reach the innermost sanctum of the temple.

Another oevent which is celebrated in Egypt is Eid Al Ghetass, the commemoration of the baptism of Jesus Christ, which is also mentioned in holy Quran. It has been celebrated since the ancient people of egypt and celebrated with traditional sweets.
